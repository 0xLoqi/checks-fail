## January 6 Pardons and Commutations

### [2025-01-20] Trump Issues Blanket Clemency for ~1,500 January 6 Defendants
WHAT: On his first day in office, President Trump signed an executive order granting full pardons to approximately 1,500 people convicted of or charged with offenses related to the January 6 Capitol attack, and commuting the sentences of 14 others including leaders of the Oath Keepers and Proud Boys.

SOURCE: [NBC News - Oath Keepers' Rhodes and Proud Boys' Tarrio released from prison after Trump Jan. 6 clemency](https://www.nbcdfw.com/news/local/oath-keepers-stewart-rhodes-released-prison-trump-clemency/3746604/)

### [2025-11-15] Trump Issues Two Additional Pardons Related to Jan. 6 Investigation
WHAT: Trump issued two additional pardons connected to the January 6 investigation, extending clemency beyond the original blanket order.
SOURCE: [NPR - President Trump issues two pardons related to Jan. 6 investigation](https://www.npr.org/2025/11/15/nx-s1-5609943/trump-two-pardons-jan-6-investigation)

## Targeting the Justice Department and Prosecutors

### [2025-01-10] Special Counsel Jack Smith Resigns Before Inauguration
WHAT: Special Counsel Jack Smith resigned from the Justice Department after submitting his final report, having previously dropped both federal criminal cases against Trump (election subversion and classified documents) following Trump's November 2024 election victory, citing the longstanding DOJ policy against prosecuting sitting presidents.
SOURCE: [NPR - Jack Smith has resigned from the justice department](https://www.npr.org/2025/01/12/g-s1-42365/jack-smith-has-resigned-from-the-justice-department-after-submitting-his-trump-report)

### [2025-01-28] DOJ Fires Prosecutors Who Worked on Trump Criminal Investigations
WHAT: The Trump administration fired career DOJ officials who worked on Special Counsel Jack Smith's investigations into Trump, including prosecutors from the election subversion and classified documents cases.
SOURCE: [NBC News - Trump administration fires DOJ officials who worked on criminal investigations of the president](https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/justice-department/trump-administration-fires-doj-officials-worked-criminal-investigation-rcna189512)

### [2025-02-05] Attorney General Bondi Establishes Weaponization Working Group
WHAT: Hours after being sworn in, AG Pam Bondi signed a memorandum creating the "Weaponization Working Group" to review Biden-era prosecutions and identify DOJ employees involved in Trump-related cases, Jan 6 prosecutions, and investigations of Trump allies.
SOURCE: [ABC News - Bondi, as new AG, launches Weaponization Working Group](https://abcnews.go.com/US/bondi-new-ag-launches-weaponization-working-group-review/story?id=118501463)

### [2025-03-01] DOJ Demotes Senior Attorneys Who Oversaw January 6 Cases
WHAT: Trump's Justice Department demoted at least seven top prosecutors in the D.C. U.S. Attorney's office who oversaw January 6 cases, with acting U.S. attorney Ed Martin (who had praised the Capitol attack) leading the effort.
SOURCE: [NPR - Trump's Justice Department demotes senior attorneys who oversaw Jan. 6 cases](https://www.npr.org/2025/03/01/nx-s1-5313836/trumps-justice-department-demotes-senior-attorneys-who-oversaw-jan-6-cases)

### [2025-03-31] Trump Administration Fires 50+ Prosecutors and Deputies in DOJ Overhaul
WHAT: The White House, in coordination with DOJ, dismissed more than 50 U.S. attorneys and deputies in what was described as a norm-shattering departure from the tradition of career prosecutors remaining across administrations.
SOURCE: [Axios - Trump admin fires 50 prosecutors and deputies in DOJ overhaul](https://www.axios.com/2025/03/31/trump-doj-prosecutors-fired)

### [2025-07-12] Bondi Fires 20+ DOJ Employees Tied to Jack Smith's Prosecutions
WHAT: AG Bondi fired more than 20 DOJ employees -- lawyers, support staff, and U.S. marshals -- who worked on Jan. 6 and classified documents cases, identified by the Weaponization Working Group, bringing total Smith-related firings to approximately 35.
SOURCE: [Axios - Bondi fires 20 DOJ employees from Jan. 6, Trump documents cases](https://www.axios.com/2025/07/12/pam-bondi-fires-justice-employees-jan6-documents-trump)

### [2025-07-16] Maurene Comey, Daughter of James Comey, Fired from SDNY
WHAT: Federal prosecutor Maurene Comey -- lead prosecutor in the Epstein/Maxwell and Sean Combs cases -- was fired from the Southern District of New York without cause, reportedly after Trump was angered that a relative of former FBI Director James Comey worked in his administration; she later sued, alleging politically motivated retaliation.
SOURCE: [CNN - Maurene Comey, federal prosecutor in Epstein case, fired from US attorney's office](https://www.cnn.com/2025/07/16/politics/maurene-comey-fired-sdny)

### [2025 Year-End Estimate] Over 230 DOJ Lawyers, Agents, and Staff Fired
WHAT: Justice Connection, a network of DOJ alumni, estimated that more than 230 lawyers, agents, and other employees were fired from across the department, apparently for their work on assigned cases or past criticism of Trump.

## Inspector General Firings

### [2025-01-24] Trump Fires 17 Inspectors General in Mass Purge
WHAT: Trump fired at least 17 inspectors general across federal agencies -- including at the Departments of Defense, State, HUD, VA, Energy, and Transportation -- via email citing "changing priorities," without providing the 30-day congressional notice required by the 1978 Inspector General Act.
SOURCE: [NPR - Trump uses mass firing to remove inspectors general at a series of agencies](https://www.npr.org/2025/01/25/g-s1-44771/trump-fires-inspectors-general)

### [2025-09-24] Federal Judge Rules IG Firings Were Unlawful
WHAT: Federal Judge Ana C. Reyes ruled that the administration unlawfully fired 17 inspectors general by failing to provide required congressional notice, but declined to reinstate them, noting Trump could simply re-fire them after complying with the 30-day notice requirement.
SOURCE: [Federal News Network - Judge finds Trump unlawfully fired agency IGs, but won't reinstate them](https://federalnewsnetwork.com/agency-oversight/2025/09/trump-unlawfully-fired-17-agency-igs-judge-finds-but-wont-reinstate-them/)

### [2025-10-XX] Trump Fires USAID Inspector General After Critical Report
WHAT: Trump fired Paul Martin, the USAID inspector general, after his office released a report detailing the potential consequences of the administration's dismantling of the agency.
SOURCE: [Government Executive - Trump fires another inspector general](https://www.govexec.com/oversight/2025/10/trump-fires-another-inspector-general-raising-fears-about-oversight-independence/408950/)

### [Ongoing] Over 75% of Presidentially Appointed IG Positions Sit Vacant
WHAT: Between Trump's firings, additional resignations, and vacancies carried over from the Biden administration, more than 75% of presidentially appointed inspector general positions are now unfilled, severely undermining federal oversight capacity.
SOURCE: [PBS News - Inspectors general fired by Trump issue warning about lack of oversight](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/show/inspectors-general-fired-by-trump-issue-warning-about-lack-of-oversight)

## Whistleblower Retaliation and Civil Service Attacks

### [2025-02-07] Trump Fires Head of Office of Special Counsel (Whistleblower Protection Agency)
WHAT: Trump fired Hampton Dellinger, the Senate-confirmed head of the Office of Special Counsel -- the independent agency responsible for investigating whistleblower retaliation and protecting federal employees -- despite federal law restricting the president's removal power to cases of "inefficiency, neglect of duty, or malfeasance."
SOURCE: [NPR - Head of federal whistleblower office drops legal battle challenging his firing](https://www.npr.org/2025/03/05/nx-s1-5319326/trump-hampton-dellinger-watchdog-appeals-court)

### [2025-03-01] Federal Judge Rules Dellinger Firing Unlawful, Orders Reinstatement
WHAT: U.S. District Judge Amy Berman Jackson ruled that Trump's firing of Dellinger was unlawful and ordered his reinstatement, but a D.C. Circuit appeals panel later sided with the administration, and Dellinger ultimately dropped his legal challenge.
SOURCE: [NPR - Head of federal whistleblower agency sues Trump over firing](https://www.npr.org/2025/02/10/nx-s1-5292259/hampton-dellinger-trump-special-counsel)

### [2026-02-05] Trump Finalizes Schedule Policy/Career Rule, Stripping Protections from 50,000 Federal Workers
WHAT: OPM finalized the "Schedule Policy/Career" rule (formerly Schedule F), reclassifying approximately 50,000 federal workers in policy-influencing positions as at-will employees who can be fired without cause, removing their civil service protections, Merit Systems Protection Board appeal rights, and independent whistleblower retaliation investigation procedures -- with 94% of 40,000+ public comments opposing the rule.
SOURCE: [NPR - New rule expands Trump's power to fire federal workers](https://www.npr.org/2026/02/06/nx-s1-5704171/trump-fire-federal-employees-schedule-f)

### [2026-02-05] Whistleblower Protections Gutted Under New Rule
WHAT: Under the finalized Schedule Policy/Career rule, reclassified employees will no longer be able to bring whistleblower retaliation complaints to the independent Office of Special Counsel; instead, individual agencies will be responsible for investigating complaints against themselves.
SOURCE: [Federal News Network - Trump administration advances plan to strip job protections from career federal employees](https://federalnewsnetwork.com/workforce/2026/02/trump-administration-advances-plan-to-strip-job-protections-from-career-federal-employees/)

## Security Clearance Revocations as Political Punishment

### [2025-01-20] Trump Revokes Clearances of 51 Former Intelligence Officials
WHAT: On his first day in office, Trump signed an executive order revoking the security clearances of 51 former intelligence officials who signed a 2020 letter suggesting the Hunter Biden laptop story bore hallmarks of a Russian information operation; those named include former DNI James Clapper, former CIA Directors John Brennan and Michael Hayden, and former Defense Secretary Leon Panetta.
SOURCE: [CNN - Security clearances revoked for former officials who signed Hunter Biden laptop letter](https://www.cnn.com/2025/01/20/politics/trump-revokes-security-clearances-former-officials-hunter-biden-laptop-letter)

### [2025-03-22] Trump Revokes Clearances of Biden, Clinton, Harris, Cheney, Vindman, and Others
WHAT: Trump signed a second executive order revoking security clearances and classified briefing access from a broader list of political opponents including Joe Biden and family, Kamala Harris, Hillary Clinton, Liz Cheney, Alexander Vindman, Fiona Hill, Adam Kinzinger, Antony Blinken, Jake Sullivan, Lisa Monaco, Letitia James, Alvin Bragg, Mark Zaid, Norman Eisen, and Andrew Weissmann.
SOURCE: [The Hill - Trump axes security clearances for political foes, including Hillary Clinton, Harris, Cheney](https://thehill.com/homenews/administration/5208695-trump-security-clearance-clinton-harris-cheney/)

### [2025-03-06] Trump Signs Executive Order Targeting Perkins Coie Law Firm
WHAT: Trump signed an executive order suspending security clearances of all employees at Perkins Coie LLP, barring them from government buildings, and canceling government contracts -- citing the firm's role in the Steele dossier and its DEI practices; a federal judge permanently struck down the order as unconstitutional.
SOURCE: [NPR - Judge strikes down Trump executive order targeting Perkins Coie law firm](https://www.npr.org/2025/05/02/nx-s1-5385355/perkins-coie-trump-executive-order-law-firms)

### [2025-03-27] Trump Signs Executive Orders Targeting WilmerHale and Other Law Firms
WHAT: Trump issued executive orders targeting WilmerHale (for its connection to Robert Mueller's investigation), Jenner & Block, and Susman Godfrey (for representing Dominion Voting Systems), revoking security clearances and barring access to government facilities; federal judges permanently blocked all four law firm executive orders as unconstitutional violations of the First, Fifth, and Sixth Amendments.
SOURCE: [CNN - Judge strikes down executive order targeting WilmerHale](https://www.cnn.com/2025/05/27/politics/law-firms-wilmerhale-executive-order)

### [2025-08-XX] Trump Administration Revokes Clearances of 37 Additional Officials

- **Political opponents under investigation:** Adam Schiff, John Brennan, Jerome Powell, Lisa Cook
- **Inspectors general fired:** 17+ (ruled unlawful by federal court); 75%+ of IG positions now vacant
- **DOJ employees fired/forced out:** 230+ lawyers, agents, and staff
- **Security clearances revoked:** 51 intelligence officials + Biden, Clinton, Harris, Cheney, Vindman, and dozens more; all four law firm executive orders struck down as unconstitutional
- **Federal workers losing civil service protections:** ~50,000 under Schedule Policy/Career rule
- **Law firms targeted by executive order:** 4 (Perkins Coie, WilmerHale, Jenner & Block, Susman Godfrey) -- all orders struck down by federal courts
