## USAID Shutdown & Humanitarian Consequences

### [2025-01-20] Executive Order Freezes All U.S. Foreign Aid
WHAT: President Trump signed Executive Order 14169, "Reevaluating and Realigning United States Foreign Aid," imposing a 90-day pause on all foreign development assistance programs.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/sections/goats-and-soda/2025/02/14/g-s1-48994/usaid-foreign-aid-freeze)

### [2025-01-24] Secretary Rubio Issues Stop-Work Orders on Foreign Aid
WHAT: Secretary of State Marco Rubio enforced the aid pause with a memorandum calling for stop-work orders on existing foreign aid contracts and grants.
SOURCE: [Axios](https://www.axios.com/2025/03/28/usaid-reorganization-department-of-state)

### [2025-02-06] Over 10,000 USAID Employees Placed on Leave
WHAT: More than 10,000 USAID employees were placed on administrative leave, leaving only a few hundred remaining staff to manage the agency.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/sections/goats-and-soda/2025/07/01/g-s1-75222/usaid-trump-humanitarian-rubio-musk)

### [2025-03-10] 83% of USAID Programs Cut
WHAT: Secretary Rubio announced the 90-day review had concluded, with 83% of USAID programs terminated, representing approximately 5,200 contracts.
SOURCE: [Axios](https://www.axios.com/2025/03/28/usaid-reorganization-department-of-state)

### [2025-07-01] USAID Officially Shuts Down
WHAT: USAID ceased operations and merged remaining programs into the State Department, ending the agency's 64-year history.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/07/01/nx-s1-5451372/usaid-officially-shuts-down-and-merges-remaining-operations-with-state-department)

### [2025-07-01] Lancet Study Projects Up to 14 Million Deaths from Aid Cuts
WHAT: A Lancet study estimated that if USAID funding cuts continue through 2030, between 8 and 19 million additional people could die, including 4.5 million children.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/sections/goats-and-soda/2025/07/01/nx-s1-5452513/trump-usaid-foreign-aid-deaths)

### [2025-07-01] UN Experts Declare U.S. "Fuelling Global Humanitarian Catastrophe"
WHAT: United Nations human rights experts issued a statement declaring the U.S. government was fuelling a global humanitarian catastrophe through its aid cuts.
SOURCE: [CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2025/02/04/world/usaid-us-foreign-aid-freeze-humanitarian-crises-intl/index.html)

### [2026-02-03] Appropriations Bill Formally Continues USAID Closure
WHAT: The 2026 Consolidated Appropriations Act signed by Trump formally continued the closure of USAID and created an "America First Opportunity Fund," cutting nearly $10 billion in foreign assistance.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/07/01/nx-s1-5451372/usaid-officially-shuts-down-and-merges-remaining-operations-with-state-department)

## CFPB Dismantling

### [2025-02-08] Acting Director Vought Orders CFPB to Stop Work
WHAT: OMB Director Russell Vought, newly installed as CFPB acting director, issued directives ordering all staff to halt supervision, investigations, enforcement, rulemaking, and stakeholder activities.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/02/08/nx-s1-5290914/russell-vought-cfpb-doge-access-musk)

### [2025-02-11] Two Top CFPB Officials Resign
WHAT: Two senior CFPB officials resigned after being ordered to stop all work at the consumer protection agency.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/02/11/nx-s1-5293165/cfpb-russell-vought)

### [2025-02-14] Federal Judge Issues Temporary Restraining Order Protecting CFPB
WHAT: A federal judge blocked the Trump administration from deleting CFPB data, terminating employees, or reducing the agency's reserve funding.
SOURCE: [PBS](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/politics/vought-orders-cfpb-to-stop-investigations-and-suspend-new-rules-from-taking-effect)

### [2025-11-11] Administration Declares CFPB Funding Unlawful
WHAT: The Trump administration declared it would consider the CFPB's funding structure unlawful and would not seek additional money to run the agency.
SOURCE: [CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2025/12/30/politics/cfbp-funding-lawsuit-rejected)

### [2025-12-30] Federal Judge Orders CFPB Must Remain Funded
WHAT: A federal judge rejected Acting Director Vought's argument that he could effectively close a congressionally-created agency without an act of Congress, ruling the CFPB must continue to be funded.
SOURCE: [CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2025/12/30/politics/cfbp-funding-lawsuit-rejected)

### [2026-01-09] Vought Requests Minimal Funding to Comply with Court Order
WHAT: CFPB Acting Director Vought requested just $145 million from the Federal Reserve, enough to keep the CFPB technically open through March 2026, in compliance with the December court order.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/01/21/nx-s1-5683060/cfpb-consumer-watchdog-agency-trump-second-term)

### [2026-01-21] 22 State Attorneys General Sue to Force CFPB to Function
WHAT: Attorneys general from 22 states and territories filed a motion asking a federal court to compel the CFPB to actually perform its statutory duties, including requesting its own operating funds.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/01/21/nx-s1-5683060/cfpb-consumer-watchdog-agency-trump-second-term)

## EPA Gutting

### [2025-02-26] Trump Announces Plans to Cut 65% of EPA
WHAT: In a cabinet meeting, President Trump stated that EPA Administrator Lee Zeldin planned to fire 65% of EPA staff; a White House official later clarified this referred to a 65% spending cut.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/05/02/nx-s1-5385272/epa-environmental-protection-agency-cuts-trump-zeldin)

### [2025-05-02] EPA Budget Proposes Cutting Agency to Reagan-Era Staffing Levels
WHAT: The Trump administration's FY2026 budget proposed reducing EPA staffing to approximately 6,000 employees, levels not seen since the Reagan administration, with the science budget cut by 52%.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/05/02/nx-s1-5385272/epa-environmental-protection-agency-cuts-trump-zeldin)

### [2025-07-18] EPA Confirms 23% of Personnel Already Cut
WHAT: An EPA press release confirmed the agency had terminated 3,707 of its 16,155 employees, a 23% reduction, and eliminated its research and development office.
SOURCE: [NBC News](https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/trump-administration/epa-eliminates-research-development-office-layoffs-begin-rcna219725)

### [2025-09-30] EPA On Track to Lose One-Third of Staff by Year's End
WHAT: Approximately 33% of EPA staffers at the time Trump took office were projected to be gone by end of 2025, leaving roughly 9,700 employees, a level not seen since the Nixon and Ford administrations.
SOURCE: [Politico/E&E News](https://www.eenews.net/articles/trump-gutted-climate-rules-in-2025-he-could-make-it-permanent-in-2026/)

### [2025-12-15] Report Finds EPA Cuts Imperil Environmental Protections Across States
WHAT: The Trump administration proposed cutting EPA "categorical grants" by approximately $1 billion, eliminating nearly all federal funding for air quality management, pollution control, brownfield management, and lead abatement.
SOURCE: [Politico/E&E News](https://www.eenews.net/articles/trump-gutted-climate-rules-in-2025-he-could-make-it-permanent-in-2026/)

### [2025-03-20] Trump Signs Executive Order to Close Education Department
WHAT: President Trump signed an executive order directing Education Secretary Linda McMahon to "take all necessary steps to facilitate the closure of the Department of Education."

### [2025-10-10] Additional 500 Education Department Employees Fired
WHAT: Nearly 500 more staffers were fired, leaving the department with just over 2,000 employees, less than half its pre-Trump workforce.
SOURCE: [CBS News](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/trump-executive-order-education-department/)

### [2025-11-18] Administration Begins Transferring Education Functions to Other Agencies
WHAT: The administration announced transfers of core functions to four other agencies: Title I and postsecondary education work to Labor, Indian education to Interior, and international education to State.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/11/18/nx-s1-5607221/education-department-trump)

## IRS Workforce Reduction

### [2025-01-28] "Fork in the Road" Deferred Resignation Offer Sent to Federal Workers
WHAT: The Office of Personnel Management sent a memo to all federal employees offering deferred resignation with pay through September 30, 2025; an estimated 4,000-5,000 IRS workers accepted.
SOURCE: [Axios](https://www.axios.com/2025/02/05/deferred-resignation-program-irs-tax-refund)

### [2025-04-04] IRS Begins Reduction in Force
WHAT: The IRS implemented a reduction in force across multiple offices, with the Office of Civil Rights and Compliance losing 75% of its staff.
SOURCE: [PBS](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/politics/watchdog-warns-trumps-budget-bill-could-complicate-2026-tax-filing-season-after-irs-staffing-cuts)

### [2025-06-30] IRS Watchdog Warns of 2026 Filing Season Collapse
WHAT: The National Taxpayer Advocate warned that taxpayer service levels could drop from 85% in the 2025 filing season to just 16% in 2026 due to the loss of 8,600 Taxpayer Services employees.
SOURCE: [PBS](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/nation/irs-faces-challenges-in-2026-tax-season-due-to-jobs-cuts-and-new-laws)

### [2025-12-31] IRS Workforce Reduced by 27%
WHAT: The IRS ended 2025 with approximately 74,000 employees, down from 102,000 at the start of the year, a 27% reduction driven by DOGE initiatives, deferred resignations, and layoffs.
SOURCE: [PBS](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/nation/irs-faces-challenges-in-2026-tax-season-due-to-jobs-cuts-and-new-laws)

## SSA / DOGE Data Access

### [2025-06-06] Supreme Court Grants DOGE Access to Social Security Data
WHAT: The Supreme Court overturned lower court injunctions and granted DOGE unfettered access to Social Security Administration records, including Social Security numbers, medical records, and family court information for over 300 million Americans.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/06/06/nx-s1-5422283/supreme-court-doge-social-security-records)

### [2025-10-XX] SSA Discovers DOGE Data Was Shared to Unauthorized Server
WHAT: An SSA records review discovered that DOGE employees had used Cloudflare, an unapproved internet services provider, to share sensitive Social Security data without agency officials' knowledge, violating security protocols.
SOURCE: [CNN](https://edition.cnn.com/2026/01/20/politics/doge-social-security-data-unauthorized-server)

### [2026-01-20] Trump Administration Admits DOGE Mishandled Social Security Data
WHAT: In a court filing, the DOJ acknowledged for the first time that DOGE employees accessed and shared sensitive Social Security data without the awareness of agency officials, and that the SSA could not determine what data was shared or whether it still exists on unauthorized servers.
SOURCE: [Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/2026/01/20/doge-social-security-data-privacy-act/)

### [2026-01-23] Investigation Reveals DOGE Employee Coordinated with Political Group on Voter Roll Matching
WHAT: An NPR investigation found that a DOGE employee at SSA signed an agreement with an unnamed political advocacy group to match Social Security data with state voter rolls to find evidence of voter fraud.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/01/23/nx-s1-5684185/doge-data-social-security-privacy)

### [2026-01-23] PBS Whistleblower Report on DOGE Data Mishandling
WHAT: A former SSA chief data officer, serving as a whistleblower, responded publicly after the DOJ confirmed that DOGE employees put the records of more than 300 million Americans at risk.
SOURCE: [PBS](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/show/whistleblower-responds-after-doj-confirms-doge-mishandled-social-security-data)

## Medicare, Medicaid & SNAP Cuts (One Big Beautiful Bill Act)

### [2025-07-01] Senate Passes "One Big Beautiful Bill" with Massive Safety Net Cuts
WHAT: Senate Republicans passed the reconciliation bill containing $911 billion in Medicaid cuts over 10 years and $186.7 billion in SNAP cuts, the largest rollback of federal health support in U.S. history.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/07/01/nx-s1-5450367/senate-republicans-trump-tax-bill-medicaid)

### [2025-07-04] Trump Signs "One Big Beautiful Bill Act" Into Law
WHAT: The law imposed national Medicaid work requirements (80 hours/month), cut Medicaid by $911 billion over 10 years, and reduced SNAP by $186.7 billion; CBO estimated 10 million people would lose health coverage and 2.4 million would lose food assistance monthly.
SOURCE: [PBS](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/show/exploring-the-potential-impact-of-medicaid-cuts-in-trumps-big-budget-bill)

### [2026-02-03] Work Requirements Begin Affecting Older Adults
WHAT: Medicaid and SNAP work requirements from the One Big Beautiful Bill Act began impacting older workers and adults approaching retirement, with implementation timelines taking effect.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/07/01/nx-s1-5450367/senate-republicans-trump-tax-bill-medicaid)

## NOAA / National Weather Service Impacts

### [2025-02-27] Trump Administration Fires 880 NOAA Employees
WHAT: The Office of Personnel Management abruptly terminated approximately 880 NOAA probationary employees, including meteorologists, radar specialists, and Hurricane Hunters crew members, giving some less than two hours to leave.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/02/27/nx-s1-5298738/trump-administration-layoffs-hit-noaa-the-agency-that-forecasts-weather-and-hurricanes)

### [2025-03-XX] Court Temporarily Reinstates Fired NOAA Workers
WHAT: A federal court blocked the mass terminations and reinstated the 800+ NOAA workers, but an appeals court later overturned the ruling, and officials fired the workers again.
SOURCE: [PBS](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/politics/fired-rehired-and-fired-again-noaa-employees-are-caught-in-a-liminal-state)

### [2025-04-11] Trump Budget Proposes 27% Cut to NOAA
WHAT: The administration's FY2026 budget proposed cutting NOAA to $4.5 billion, a 27% ($1.7 billion) reduction, including zeroing out the Office of Oceanic and Atmospheric Research and all NOAA weather laboratories.
SOURCE: [CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2025/04/11/climate/trump-noaa-budget-cuts)

### [2025-06-26] National Weather Service Discontinues Key Weather Models
WHAT: The NWS announced discontinuation of the North American Mesoscale Model (NAM), High Resolution Ensemble Forecast (HREF), and other key forecasting models used by meteorologists.
SOURCE: [CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2026/01/14/climate/noaa-nasa-funding-congress-trump-climate)

### [2025-XX-XX] NWS Forecast Office Stops 24/7 Operations
WHAT: The National Weather Service office in Goodland, Kansas became the first NWS office to stop operating 24 hours a day, with about a dozen more expected to follow.
SOURCE: [Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/weather/2025/03/01/trump-firings-noaa-nws-weather-forecast-impacts/)

### [2026-01-14] Congress Moves to Reject NOAA Cuts
WHAT: Congressional appropriators moved to provide NOAA approximately $6.17 billion, roughly the same as 2025 funding, and $634 million for its research office that the administration had zeroed out.
SOURCE: [CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2026/01/14/climate/noaa-nasa-funding-congress-trump-climate)

## CDC / Public Health

### [2025-05-02] Trump Budget Proposes 53% Cut to CDC
WHAT: The FY2026 budget proposed slashing the CDC from approximately $9.2 billion to $4.3 billion, a 53% cut, with a 43% reduction in staff from 13,363 to 7,571 employees.
SOURCE: [PBS](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/politics/watchdog-warns-trumps-budget-bill-could-complicate-2026-tax-filing-season-after-irs-staffing-cuts)

### [2025-10-XX] CDC Loses 24% of Employees Through Layoffs and Buyouts
WHAT: Roughly 24% of CDC employees were eliminated through a combination of reduction-in-force notices, earlier layoffs, and voluntary early retirement offers.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/02/04/nx-s1-5699465/congress-fully-funds-health-agencies-restoring-rfk-jr-s-cuts)

### [2025-XX-XX] CDC Chronic Disease and Injury Prevention Centers Targeted for Elimination
WHAT: The administration proposed eliminating the National Center for Chronic Disease Prevention, National Center for Environmental Health, and National Center for Injury Prevention and Control, along with the $1.4 billion chronic disease center.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/02/04/nx-s1-5699465/congress-fully-funds-health-agencies-restoring-rfk-jr-s-cuts)

### [2026-01-24] CDC Abruptly Pauses $5 Billion in Public Health Grants to States
WHAT: The CDC notified states and localities that $5.1 billion in public health infrastructure grants funding health departments in all 50 states, 8 territories, and 48 large localities had been paused; the pause was reversed within 24 hours after outcry.
SOURCE: [CNN](https://edition.cnn.com/2026/01/24/health/cdc-grant-pause-reversal)

### [2026-01-24] Washington Post Reports on Public Health Funding Confusion
WHAT: The freeze and rapid reversal of public health funds sowed confusion among state and local health departments that depend on CDC grants for lab testing, emergency preparedness, and direct patient care.
SOURCE: [Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/health/2026/01/24/public-health-trump-kennedy-cdc/)

### [2026-02-04] Congress Fully Funds Health Agencies, Rejecting RFK Jr.'s Cuts
WHAT: The bipartisan 2026 appropriations bill fully funded the CDC and other health agencies, restoring cuts that Trump and HHS Secretary Robert F. Kennedy Jr. had attempted to implement.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/02/04/nx-s1-5699465/congress-fully-funds-health-agencies-restoring-rfk-jr-s-cuts)

## Federal Research Grants Frozen

WHAT: The Trump administration imposed restrictions at the NIH including cancellation of grant review panels, a communications pause, a hiring freeze, and an indefinite ban on travel.

WHAT: OMB ordered a freeze on federal grant disbursements; a federal court issued an emergency stay before the freeze deadline, but NIH did not publish grant review meeting notices between January 22 and March 3, 2025.

### [2025-XX-XX] 5,844 NIH Grants and 1,996 NSF Grants Cancelled or Suspended
WHAT: A total of 7,840 federal research grants across NIH and NSF were cancelled or suspended, with the administration proposing to cut NIH by more than 40% and NSF by nearly 57% in FY2026.

### [2025-10-01 to 2025-11-12] Government Shutdown Halts All New Grant Awards
WHAT: During the 43-day government shutdown, all new federal research grant awards and application reviews were paused across all agencies.
SOURCE: [NBC News](https://www.nbcnews.com/science/science-news/trump-science-research-funding-cuts-congress-rebuffed-rcna256793)

### [2026-02-03] Congress Restores Most Science Funding
WHAT: The FY2026 appropriations bill provided NIH with $48.7 billion (a $415 million increase over 2025) and NSF with only a 3.4% cut, largely rebuffing the administration's proposed decimation of research funding.

WHAT: A partial government shutdown began after the Senate failed to advance a funding package in a 45-55 vote, with OMB initiating shutdown procedures at midnight.

### [2026-02-03] Second Shutdown Ends as Trump Signs Appropriations Bill
