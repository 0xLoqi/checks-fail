# Press Freedom, Transparency, and Democratic Norms Under the Trump Administration (2025-2026)

Research compiled: February 8, 2026
Sources: NPR, PBS, CNN, NBC, CBS, AP, Reuters, ProPublica, Axios, Politico, CPJ, NYT, WaPo

## Press Credential Revocations and Access Restrictions

### [2025-02-11] Associated Press Barred from White House Events
WHAT: The White House revoked AP reporters' access to presidential events after the news agency refused to adopt the administration's "Gulf of America" renaming of the Gulf of Mexico.
SOURCE: [NBC News](https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/trump-administration/associated-press-white-house-access-rcna193531)

### [2025-02-08] Pentagon Evicts News Organizations from Press Workspaces
WHAT: The Trump administration doubled the number of news organizations evicted from Pentagon workspaces, removing CNN, The Washington Post, The Hill, and War Zone and replacing them with Newsmax, the Washington Examiner, the Daily Caller, and The Free Press.
SOURCE: [PBS NewsHour](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/show/why-news-organizations-are-rejecting-the-pentagons-new-press-rules)

### [2025-02-21] Associated Press Files Lawsuit Over Access Ban
WHAT: The AP filed a First Amendment lawsuit against three Trump administration officials over the unconstitutional termination of its White House press access.
SOURCE: [PBS NewsHour](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/politics/judge-wont-immediately-restore-associated-press-access-to-white-house)

### [2025-02-26] White House Seizes Control of Press Pool
WHAT: After more than a century of journalist self-governance, the White House Correspondents' Association ceded control of the press pool to the Trump administration, giving the White House power to decide which journalists and outlets cover the president.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/02/26/nx-s1-5308628/trump-white-house-press-access-voa)

### [2025-04-08] Judge Orders White House to Restore AP Access
WHAT: A federal judge ruled the White House ban on the Associated Press violated the First Amendment and ordered the administration to restore the outlet's access to presidential events.
SOURCE: [PBS NewsHour](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/politics/judge-wont-immediately-restore-associated-press-access-to-white-house)

### [2025-05-24] Pentagon Implements New Press Restrictions
WHAT: Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth announced new restraints requiring Pentagon journalists to agree not to gather any information -- even unclassified material -- that has not been expressly authorized for release.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/05/24/nx-s1-5410513/defense-sec-hegseth-press-access-pentagon)

### [2025-06-06] Appeals Court Reinstates AP Oval Office Ban
WHAT: A federal appeals court panel reversed the lower court's order, allowing the White House to reinstate its ban of the AP from the Oval Office, Air Force One, and Mar-a-Lago while the case continues.
SOURCE: [NBC News](https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/trump-administration/associated-press-white-house-access-rcna193531)

### [2025-09-19] Pentagon Demands Journalists Sign Pledge Against Obtaining Unauthorized Material
WHAT: The Pentagon announced it would require reporters to pledge they would not gather unauthorized information and would revoke credentials of those who refuse.
SOURCE: [Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/business/2025/09/19/pentagon-hegseth-press-unauthorized-material/)

### [2025-10-15] Mass Journalist Exodus from Pentagon
WHAT: Reporters from virtually every major media outlet surrendered their Pentagon press passes and left the building in a unified rejection of Hegseth's new credentialing restrictions; only One America News publicly agreed to the terms.
SOURCE: [CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2025/10/15/media/pentagon-press-hegseth-restrictions-journalists-fox)

### [2025-10-31] White House Restricts Press Office Access
WHAT: The White House limited reporters' access to the press secretary's office, citing the presence of sensitive materials.
SOURCE: [CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2025/11/01/business/trump-white-house-press-limits)

## Journalist Threats, Detention, and Lawsuits

### [2025-01-20] January 6 Blanket Pardons Including Those Who Attacked Journalists
WHAT: President Trump granted blanket clemency to nearly 1,600 people convicted of or facing trial for January 6 offenses, including individuals who had been convicted of assaulting members of the press during the Capitol attack.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/01/20/g-s1-36809/trump-pardons-january-6-riot)

### [2025-04-25] DOJ Rescinds Protections Against Subpoenaing Journalists
WHAT: Attorney General Pam Bondi rescinded Biden-era policies that protected reporters from being subpoenaed to reveal their confidential sources, stating that the news media "must answer subpoenas" to help the administration identify and punish leakers.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/04/25/nx-s1-5377624/pam-bondi-reporters-subpoena-leaks)

### [2025-04-25] DOJ Rescinds Journalist Protections (Analysis)
WHAT: Bondi's memo stated the DOJ "will not tolerate unauthorized disclosures that undermine President Donald Trump's policies," reversing a policy that press freedom advocates said had enabled consequential reporting from Watergate to warrantless wiretapping.
SOURCE: [Axios](https://www.axios.com/2025/04/25/bondi-doj-garland-subpoena-journalists)

### [2025-07-02] Paramount Settles Trump Lawsuit for $16 Million
WHAT: Paramount Global agreed to pay $16 million to settle Trump's lawsuit alleging CBS's "60 Minutes" deceptively edited a Kamala Harris interview, a settlement press freedom advocates called an act of corporate capitulation to presidential power.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/07/02/nx-s1-5454790/cbs-settlement-trump-60-minutes-harris-interview-analysis)

### [2025-08-25] Trump Calls for FCC to Revoke ABC and NBC Licenses
WHAT: President Trump publicly called for the FCC to revoke the broadcast licenses of ABC and NBC, accusing them of being "two of the worst and most biased networks in history."
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/08/25/nx-s1-5515480/fcc-abc-nbc-reviews)

### [2025-10-03] Journalist Mario Guevara Deported to El Salvador
WHAT: Live-streaming journalist Mario Guevara, who had been arrested while reporting on a protest and detained by ICE for 110 days including 22 hours per day in solitary confinement, was deported to El Salvador despite holding a valid U.S. work permit.
SOURCE: [CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2025/10/03/media/mario-guevara-journalist-deported-ice-el-salvador)

### [2025-11-18] Trump Calls for ABC License Revocation Over Epstein Question
WHAT: President Trump called for ABC's broadcast license to be revoked after a reporter asked him about the Jeffrey Epstein files.
SOURCE: [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/18/trump-epstein-files-abc-license.html)

## FCC Investigations and Broadcast Threats

### [2025-01 through 2025] FCC Chair Launches Politically Motivated Investigations
WHAT: FCC Chair Brendan Carr opened formal investigations into CBS, ABC, NBC, NPR, and PBS -- every major broadcast network except Fox -- including probes into NPR and PBS for alleged violations of underwriting rules and DEI investigations into Disney (ABC parent) and Comcast (NBC parent).
SOURCE: [CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2025/05/05/media/fcc-cbs-investigation)

### [2025-05-05] FCC Commissioner Calls CBS Investigation a "Penalty"
WHAT: FCC Commissioner Anna Gomez publicly characterized the FCC investigation into CBS as a "penalty" imposed for content the administration disliked, and stated "the FCC doesn't have the authority, the ability, or the constitutional right to revoke a license because of content."
SOURCE: [CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2025/05/05/media/fcc-cbs-investigation)

## Defunding Public Media and International Broadcasters

### [2025-05-01] Executive Order to Defund NPR and PBS
WHAT: President Trump signed Executive Order 14290, "Ending Taxpayer Subsidization of Biased Media," directing the Corporation for Public Broadcasting and other federal agencies to cease all federal funding for NPR and PBS, alleging biased news coverage.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/05/02/nx-s1-5384784/president-trump-orders-end-to-federal-funding-for-npr-and-pbs)

### [2025-05-27] NPR Sues Over Federal Funding Cuts
WHAT: NPR and three public radio stations filed a First Amendment lawsuit against the Trump administration challenging the executive order cutting their federal funding.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/12/05/g-s1-100844/npr-battles-trump-executive-order-in-court)

### [2025-07-18] Congress Rescinds $1.1 Billion in Public Broadcasting Funding
WHAT: Congress approved the Trump administration's plan to rescind $1.1 billion in previously allocated Corporation for Public Broadcasting funding, cutting all federal support for NPR, PBS, and their more than 1,500 local member stations.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/07/18/nx-s1-5469912/npr-congress-rescission-funding-trump)

### [2025-08-01] Corporation for Public Broadcasting Announces Shutdown
WHAT: Following the congressional funding rescission, the Corporation for Public Broadcasting announced it was beginning to wind down its operations.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/08/01/nx-s1-5489808/cpb-shut-down-public-broadcasting-trump)

### [2025-03-15] "Bloody Saturday" -- VOA and Radio Free Asia Mass Suspensions
WHAT: Over 1,000 employees and contractors at Voice of America and the Office for Cuba Broadcasting were suspended following a late-night White House order; VOA stopped broadcasting for the first time in 83 years the following day.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/03/15/nx-s1-5329244/bloody-saturday-voiceofamerica-radio-free-asia-europe-trump-kari-lake)

### [2025-04-22] Judge Blocks Dismantling of Voice of America
WHAT: Judge Royce C. Lamberth ordered the Trump administration to return VOA employees and contractors to their pre-suspension status.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/04/22/nx-s1-5372530/voa-trump-radio-free-asia)

### [2025-05-02] Radio Free Asia Mass Layoffs
WHAT: Radio Free Asia laid off most of its staff amid an ongoing funding battle with the Trump administration, including termination of Tibetan, Burmese, and Uyghur language services.
SOURCE: [CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2025/05/02/media/radio-free-asia-lay-offs-amid-trump-funding-battle)

### [2025-06-06] VOA Planned Reduction from 1,300 to 81 Workers
WHAT: Trump senior adviser Kari Lake, overseeing the downsizing, announced plans to reduce Voice of America staff from 1,300 to 81 employees.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/06/06/nx-s1-5424581/voa-job-cuts-kari-lake)

### [2025-10-31] Radio Free Asia Halts News Operations
WHAT: Radio Free Asia announced it would cease news operations entirely due to the financial crisis caused by Trump administration funding cuts.
SOURCE: [Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/business/2025/10/29/radio-free-asia-funding-crisis/)

## FOIA Obstruction and Transparency Erosion

### [2025-01-25] DOJ Removes January 6 Case Database
WHAT: The Department of Justice removed its public database tracking all January 6 prosecutions following Trump's blanket pardons, an action that government watchdog CREW said appeared to violate federal records law (44 U.S.C. 3106).
SOURCE: [CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2025/01/25/politics/january-6-justice-department-database/index.html)

### [2025-02-07] Trump Fires Archivist of the United States
WHAT: President Trump dismissed Colleen Shogan, the Archivist of the United States and first woman to hold the role, without providing a reason and without following the legal requirement to notify Congress.
SOURCE: [CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2025/02/07/politics/colleen-shogan-trump-dismisses-national-archivist/index.html)

### [2025-02-11] January 6 Video Evidence Disappears from Public Access
WHAT: A media coalition reported that video exhibits from January 6 cases had disappeared from the DOJ's public platform; a judge ordered the government to halt further removals and provide explanations.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/02/11/nx-s1-5293447/jan-6-evidence-captiol-riot-donald-trump)

### [2025-02-21] POGO Challenges DOGE Records Secrecy
WHAT: The Project On Government Oversight filed a civil complaint challenging the claim that DOGE records are covered by the Presidential Records Act, which would shield them from public access.
SOURCE: [POGO](https://www.pogo.org/policy-letters/pogo-requests-that-white-house-counsel-preserves-doge-records)

### [2025-03] FOIA Officers Fired Across Federal Agencies
WHAT: FOIA offices at the CDC, FDA, NIH, Department of Education, USAID, Consumer Financial Protection Bureau, and Office of Personnel Management were shuttered or had their staffs dramatically reduced, with DOJ FOIA chief Bobak Talebian also fired.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/sections/shots-health-news/2025/04/03/g-s1-57888/hhs-fda-rfk-foia-public-records)

### [2025-03] Judge Orders DOGE to Comply with FOIA Requests
WHAT: A federal judge ruled that DOGE must comply with Freedom of Information Act requests, rejecting the Trump administration's argument that its records should be shielded from public disclosure.
SOURCE: [Government Executive](https://www.govexec.com/oversight/2025/03/judge-orders-doge-comply-foia-requests/403660/)

### [2025-03] Congressional Democrats Expose FOIA Gutting at 24 Agencies
WHAT: Rep. Gerald E. Connolly sent letters to 24 federal agencies exposing the Trump administration for eliminating staff responsible for processing FOIA requests, jeopardizing Americans' legal right to access government information.
SOURCE: [House Oversight Democrats](https://oversightdemocrats.house.gov/news/press-releases/oversight-democrats-expose-trump-administration-undermining-foia-and-preventing)

## Epstein Files Release and Redactions

### [2025-11-19] Epstein Files Transparency Act Signed
WHAT: President Trump signed the Epstein Files Transparency Act requiring the Justice Department to release Epstein-related files by December 19, 2025.
SOURCE: [PBS NewsHour](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/show/whats-revealed-in-the-latest-epstein-files-release-and-whats-redacted)

### [2025-12-19] Initial Epstein Files Release Draws Bipartisan Criticism
WHAT: The DOJ's initial release of Epstein files on the statutory deadline drew bipartisan criticism for extensive redactions, with over 500 pages entirely blacked out and faulty redaction techniques that allowed public recovery of supposedly hidden content.
SOURCE: [PBS NewsHour](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/show/whats-revealed-in-the-latest-epstein-files-release-and-whats-redacted)

### [2026-01-30] DOJ Releases 3.5 Million Pages of Epstein Documents
WHAT: The Department of Justice published 3.5 million responsive pages in compliance with the Epstein Files Transparency Act, though redaction errors unintentionally revealed identities of Epstein's victims across separate documents.
SOURCE: [NBC News](https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/justice-department/live-blog/epstein-files-trump-doj-release-live-updates-rcna256639)

### [2026-02-01] Attorneys for 200+ Victims Demand Takedown of Epstein Files Website
WHAT: Attorneys for more than 200 alleged Epstein victims asked federal judges to order the immediate removal of the DOJ's Epstein Files website, calling the release "the single most egregious violation of victim privacy in one day in United States history" due to redaction failures.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/02/06/nx-s1-5702692/epstein-files-doj-trump-clinton-oversight)

## Federal Website Data Removal and Scientific Data Suppression

### [2025-01-20 onward] Mass Removal of Federal Government Web Content
WHAT: Beginning on Inauguration Day, government organizations removed or modified over 8,000 web pages and approximately 3,000 datasets, primarily affecting content on DEI initiatives, gender identity, public health research, environmental policy, and social programs.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/08/08/nx-s1-5495338/climate-change-environment-websites-trump)

### [2025-01-31] CDC Orders Retraction of Research Papers with Gender-Related Terms
WHAT: The CDC ordered agency scientists to retract or pause publication of research manuscripts containing terms related to gender and sexuality specified as "forbidden" by the Trump administration, and instructed co-authors to remove their names from external papers that could not be revised to align with executive orders.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/sections/shots-health-news/2025/02/13/nx-s1-5297177/cdc-scientists-publications-trump-administration)

### [2025-01-31] Trump Administration Purges Federal Health Agency Websites
WHAT: The Trump administration removed web pages and content across the CDC, FDA, NIH, and other health agencies, including information on reproductive health, LGBTQ health, and health equity research.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/sections/shots-health-news/2025/01/31/nx-s1-5282274/trump-administration-purges-health-websites)

### [2025-02-06] Climate and Environmental Data Scrubbed from Federal Websites
WHAT: In the first two weeks of the second term, the administration began removing critical environmental resources and datasets from federal agency websites, with 70% more changes to environmental websites in the first 100 days than during the first Trump administration.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/08/08/nx-s1-5495338/climate-change-environment-websites-trump)

### [2025-04] NIH Data Repositories Placed "Under Review"
WHAT: Over 20 NIH data repositories containing petabytes of public health research data -- including cancer, brain imaging, Alzheimer's, COVID-19, and HIV research -- displayed headers stating they were "under review for potential modification."
SOURCE: [KFF](https://www.kff.org/racial-equity-and-health-policy/disappearing-federal-data-implications-for-addressing-health-disparities/)

### [2025 ongoing] Over 2,000 Datasets Removed from Data.gov
WHAT: More than 2,000 datasets disappeared from Data.gov, the largest U.S. government open data repository, while DOGE canceled contracts for scheduled data gathering at the Social Security Administration and Education Department, with many contracts containing data deletion clauses.
SOURCE: [Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/opinions/interactive/2025/government-data-trump-deletion/)

### [2025 ongoing] CDC Web Pages Mass Alteration
WHAT: The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention saw over 3,000 web pages altered or removed, and the Census Bureau removed approximately 3,000 pages of research materials.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/08/08/nx-s1-5495338/climate-change-environment-websites-trump)

### [2025-01-21] HHS Communication Freeze on Health Agencies
WHAT: The Trump administration directed the CDC, FDA, NIH, and other HHS agencies to immediately pause all external communications, including health advisories, weekly scientific reports, website updates, social media posts, and public speaking engagements.

WHAT: The Trump administration imposed layered oversight requiring all CDC research publications to undergo executive branch review before release, effectively giving political appointees veto power over scientific findings.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/sections/shots-health-news/2025/02/13/nx-s1-5297177/cdc-scientists-publications-trump-administration)

### [2025 ongoing] USDA Bans Collaboration with Scientists from "Countries of Concern"
WHAT: The USDA instructed employees to stop collaborating on or publishing research papers with scientists from China, Cuba, Iran, North Korea, Russia, and Venezuela.
SOURCE: [ProPublica](https://www.propublica.org/article/trump-usda-foreign-scientists)

## Attacks on Protest Rights

### [2025-01-30] Executive Order Targeting Campus Protesters
WHAT: President Trump signed an executive order targeting "antisemitism" that authorized canceling visas and deporting foreign students deemed "Hamas sympathizers" for pro-Palestinian advocacy on college campuses.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/01/30/g-s1-45468/trump-antisemitism-executive-order-protests-deport-hamas)

### [2025-03] Trump Threatens to Cut Federal Funding for Schools Allowing "Illegal Protests"
WHAT: President Trump wrote on Truth Social that "All Federal Funding will STOP for any College, School, or University that allows illegal protests," seeking to conflate constitutionally protected protest with illegal activity.
SOURCE: [CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2025/03/13/us/protests-legal-illegal-constitution-trump/index.html)

### [2025-03-25] State Department Social Media Surveillance Directive for Visa Holders
WHAT: The State Department issued a directive declaring visa applicants ineligible if their social media showed advocacy or sympathy for designated foreign terrorist organizations, leading to over 1,000 student visa revocations by April 2025.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/04/08/nx-s1-5349472/students-protest-trump-free-speech-arrests-deportation-gaza)

### [2025-03-25] Columbia Graduate Student Mahmoud Khalil Detained
WHAT: Columbia University graduate student and legal permanent resident Mahmoud Khalil was arrested at his university housing and detained by ICE for his peaceful protests against Israel's war in Gaza, in a case that became a landmark test of First Amendment protections for non-citizens.
SOURCE: [CBS News](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/student-protesters-visas-green-cards-trump-ice-detentions-free-speech/)

### [2025-04-17] Over 1,000 Student Visas Revoked for Political Activity
WHAT: By mid-April, over 1,000 international students and graduates had their visas revoked or status terminated for pro-Palestinian advocacy, with the administration using the Canary Mission website to identify targets.
SOURCE: [NBC News](https://www.nbcnews.com/news/us-news/trump-administration-takes-aim-immigrant-students-rcna198346)

## Press Freedom Assessments

### [2025-04-30] CPJ Report: "Alarm Bells" on Press Freedom
WHAT: The Committee to Protect Journalists released its report "Alarm bells: Trump's first 100 days ramp up fear for the press, democracy," finding that press freedom is "no longer a given in the United States" and that CPJ provided safety training to over 530 journalists from November 2024 to March 2025, compared to only 20 in all of 2022.
SOURCE: [CPJ](https://cpj.org/2025/04/trumps-first-100-days-portend-long-lasting-damage-to-press-freedom/)

### [2025-05-02] U.S. Falls to 57th on World Press Freedom Index
WHAT: The U.S. fell to 57th out of 180 countries on Reporters Without Borders' World Press Freedom Index -- its lowest ranking since 2002 and the second consecutive year classified as "problematic" -- ranking below formerly war-torn Sierra Leone.
SOURCE: [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/05/05/nx-s1-5384970/press-freedom-no-longer-a-given-in-u-s-according-to-cpj-report)

### [2025-05-03] Analysis: Trump Attempting to Chill Investigative Journalism
WHAT: Analysis on World Press Freedom Day documented 215 anti-media posts by Trump on social media, including insults and threats against individual journalists and outlets, with physical assaults on reporters in 2025 nearly equaling the total from the prior three years combined.
SOURCE: [CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2025/05/03/media/analysis-trump-world-press-freedom-day-chill-journalism)
