# Immigration Enforcement & Civil Rights Violations (2025-2026)

*Research compiled: February 8, 2026*

## Executive Actions & Policy Changes

### [2025-01-20] Trump Signs Executive Orders on Immigration on Inauguration Day
WHAT: President Trump signed multiple executive orders declaring a national emergency at the southern border, deploying military troops, ending the sensitive locations policy that protected churches/schools/hospitals from ICE enforcement, and issuing an executive order to end birthright citizenship.
SOURCE: [NPR - Trump's Week One Orders on Immigration Law, Explained](https://www.npr.org/2025/01/20/nx-s1-5331857/alien-enemies-act-trump-deportations)

### [2025-01-20] Sensitive Locations Policy Rescinded
WHAT: The Trump administration rescinded the Biden-era "protected areas" policy that had shielded churches, schools, hospitals, playgrounds, domestic violence shelters, food pantries, and disaster response sites from immigration enforcement since the Obama era, replacing it with a vague directive giving ICE agents discretion to enforce in all locations.
SOURCE: [NILC - Factsheet: Trump's Rescission of Protected Areas Policies](https://www.nilc.org/resources/factsheet-trumps-rescission-of-protected-areas-policies-undermines-safety-for-all/)

### [2025-01-20] Birthright Citizenship Executive Order Signed
WHAT: Trump signed Executive Order 14160 attempting to end birthright citizenship for children born in the U.S. to undocumented immigrants or those on temporary visas, directly contradicting the 14th Amendment; the order was blocked by four federal judges before taking effect.
SOURCE: [ACLU - Trump's Birthright Citizenship Executive Order](https://www.aclu.org/news/immigrants-rights/trumps-birthright-citizenship-executive-order-what-happens-next)

### [2025-02-05] Federal Judge Blocks Birthright Citizenship Order Nationwide
WHAT: U.S. District Judge Deborah Boardman issued a nationwide preliminary injunction blocking the birthright citizenship executive order, ruling it "conflicts with the plain language of the 14th Amendment, contradicts 125-year-old binding Supreme Court precedent and runs counter to our nation's 250-year history of citizenship by birth."
SOURCE: [SCOTUSblog - Supreme Court Agrees to Hear Trump's Challenge to Birthright Citizenship](https://www.scotusblog.com/2025/12/supreme-court-agrees-to-hear-trumps-challenge-to-birthright-citizenship/)

### [2025-12-05] Supreme Court Agrees to Hear Birthright Citizenship Case
WHAT: The Supreme Court agreed to hear the Trump administration's appeal of lower court rulings blocking the birthright citizenship executive order, scheduling oral arguments for spring 2026.
SOURCE: [SCOTUSblog - Supreme Court Agrees to Hear Trump's Challenge](https://www.scotusblog.com/2025/12/supreme-court-agrees-to-hear-trumps-challenge-to-birthright-citizenship/)

## Military Deployment at the Border

### [2025-01-22] 1,500 Active-Duty Troops Deployed to Southern Border
WHAT: The Pentagon surged approximately 1,500 active-duty troops (1,000 Army soldiers, ~500 Marines) to the southern border under Trump's orders to assist with deportation flights and barrier construction.
SOURCE: [Military.com - Trump Orders 1,500 Active-Duty Troops to Border](https://www.military.com/daily-news/2025/01/22/1500-active-duty-troops-being-deployed-border-under-orders-trump.html)

### [2025-03-01] 3,000 Additional Troops Sent to Border
WHAT: Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth ordered approximately 3,000 more active-duty troops to the U.S.-Mexico border, including a Stryker brigade combat team and a general support aviation battalion, bringing the total to over 11,000 troops (6,000+ active duty plus ~5,000 National Guard).
SOURCE: [Military.com - Pentagon Sending About 3,000 More Active-Duty Troops to Border](https://www.military.com/daily-news/2025/03/01/pentagon-sending-about-3000-more-active-duty-troops-us-mexico-border.html)

### [2025-07-25] Border Troop Deployments Triple
WHAT: U.S. troop deployments at the Mexican border tripled to 7,600 active-duty personnel, now including every branch of the military in an expanded enforcement role.
SOURCE: [CNN - US Military Ordering Thousands More Troops to Southern Border](https://www.cnn.com/2025/01/22/politics/us-military-troops-southern-border/index.html)

## Alien Enemies Act & CECOT Deportations

### [2025-03-15] Trump Invokes Alien Enemies Act of 1798 for Deportations
WHAT: Trump invoked the 1798 Alien Enemies Act -- a wartime authority never before used outside of declared wars -- to rapidly deport alleged Venezuelan gang members (Tren de Aragua), claiming the flow of gang members constituted an "invasion."
SOURCE: [NPR - Alien Enemies Act: Appeals Court Blocks Trump's Deportation Tool](https://www.npr.org/2025/03/18/nx-s1-5331857/alien-enemies-act-trump-deportations)

### [2025-03-15] 238 Venezuelans Deported to CECOT Prison in El Salvador
WHAT: Three planes carrying over 260 deportees (238 Venezuelans, plus Salvadorans) were flown to El Salvador and transferred to CECOT, a maximum-security mega-prison notorious for torture, solitary confinement, and severe human rights abuses, under a $6 million/year agreement with President Bukele.
SOURCE: [CBS News - What Deported Venezuelans Endured at CECOT](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/what-deported-venezuelans-endured-at-cecot-60-minutes/)

### [2025-03-15] Judge Boasberg Orders Deportation Flights Turned Around -- Administration Defies Order
WHAT: U.S. District Judge James Boasberg ordered the administration to turn around planes carrying alleged gang members being deported under the Alien Enemies Act; the flights continued to El Salvador in defiance of the order, and deportees were imprisoned at CECOT.
SOURCE: [CNN - Boasberg Finds Probable Cause to Hold Trump Administration in Contempt](https://www.cnn.com/2025/04/16/politics/boasberg-contempt-deportation-flights/index.html)

### [2025-03-15] Kilmar Abrego Garcia Wrongfully Deported to CECOT
WHAT: Kilmar Abrego Garcia, a Salvadoran man living in Maryland, was deported to CECOT despite a 2019 immigration judge order explicitly prohibiting his removal to El Salvador due to a "clear probability of future persecution"; the government later admitted this was an "administrative error."
SOURCE: [NPR - Kilmar Abrego Garcia Granted Release from ICE](https://www.npr.org/2025/12/11/nx-s1-5640861/kilmar-abrego-garcia-granted-release-ice)

### [2025-04-07] Supreme Court Allows Alien Enemies Act Enforcement to Continue
WHAT: The Supreme Court allowed Trump to continue enforcing the Alien Enemies Act for rapid deportations while lower court litigation continued, but found the administration violated due process by providing only ~24 hours notice without information on how to exercise appeal rights.
SOURCE: [CNN - Supreme Court Allows Trump to Enforce Alien Enemies Act](https://www.cnn.com/2025/04/07/politics/supreme-court-deportation-flights-trump/index.html)

### [2025-04-10] Supreme Court Rules Government Must Facilitate Abrego Garcia's Return
WHAT: In a unanimous decision, the Supreme Court ruled the government must "facilitate" Abrego Garcia's release from CECOT and return, affirming that his deportation violated the 2019 court order protecting him; Chief Justice Roberts authored the opinion.
SOURCE: [CNBC - Supreme Court Rules U.S. Must Facilitate Return of Abrego Garcia](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/04/10/supreme-court-trump-kilmar-abrego-garcia.html)

### [2025-04-16] Judge Finds Probable Cause for Criminal Contempt Against Administration
WHAT: Judge Boasberg ruled in a 46-page decision that "probable cause exists" to hold Trump administration officials in criminal contempt for "willful disregard" of his order halting Alien Enemies Act deportation flights in March.
SOURCE: [CNBC - Judge Finds Probable Cause to Hold Trump Administration in Criminal Contempt](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/04/16/trump-deportation-case-contempt-judge.html)

### [2025-12-08] Contempt Probe Expanded to Include DHS Secretary Noem
WHAT: Judge Boasberg expanded the criminal contempt probe after the DOJ revealed that DHS Secretary Kristi Noem personally made the final call to continue deportation flights after the judge ordered them halted.
SOURCE: [CNN - Judge Expands Criminal Contempt Probe, Saying Kristi Noem Failed to Provide Answers](https://www.cnn.com/2025/12/08/politics/kristi-noem-deportation-flights-contempt)

### [2025-12-11] Kilmar Abrego Garcia Finally Released from ICE Custody
WHAT: After being returned from CECOT to U.S. immigration detention, Abrego Garcia was released after a federal judge ordered his immediate release, finding he had been "re-detained, again without lawful authority"; he returned home to Maryland nine months after his wrongful deportation.
SOURCE: [NPR - Kilmar Abrego Garcia Granted Release from ICE](https://www.npr.org/2025/12/11/nx-s1-5640861/kilmar-abrego-garcia-granted-release-ice)

## CECOT Prison Conditions

### [2025-03-20] Human Rights Watch Documents CECOT Torture and Abuse
WHAT: Human Rights Watch submitted a declaration detailing CECOT conditions including routine beatings upon arrival and during detention, forced stress positions (24 hours on knees), completely dark solitary confinement cells called "the Island," and denial of basic necessities; deportees described conditions as "You're in hell."
SOURCE: [Human Rights Watch - Declaration on Prison Conditions in El Salvador](https://www.hrw.org/news/2025/03/20/human-rights-watch-declaration-prison-conditions-el-salvador-jgg-v-trump-case)

## Student Visa Revocations & Academic Freedom

### [2025-03-08] Mahmoud Khalil, Columbia University Student, Detained by ICE
WHAT: Mahmoud Khalil, a lawful permanent resident (green card holder) and graduate student at Columbia University who served as a lead negotiator in pro-Palestinian protests, was taken from his campus apartment by ICE agents and transferred to a detention facility in Louisiana.
SOURCE: [NPR - Mahmoud Khalil Can Be Deported, Judge Rules](https://www.npr.org/2025/04/11/nx-s1-5361208/mahmoud-khalil-deported-judge-rubio-antisemitism-immigration-court)

### [2025-03-25] State Department Directive Targets Student Political Speech
WHAT: The State Department issued a directive declaring visa applicants ineligible if their social media showed them "advocating for, sympathizing with, or persuading others to endorse or espouse terrorist activities," a definition critics say is being used to target pro-Palestinian speech on campuses.
SOURCE: [The Marshall Project - Trump Targeting International Students Over Pro-Palestinian Protests](https://www.themarshallproject.org/2025/04/05/visa-immigration-first-amendment-protest-speech)

### [2025-03-25] Rumeysa Ozturk, Tufts Doctoral Student, Arrested by Masked Agents
WHAT: Rumeysa Ozturk, a Turkish doctoral candidate at Tufts University, was seized by six masked plainclothes DHS agents outside her home after her student visa was revoked for co-authoring a 2024 op-ed calling on Tufts to divest from companies with ties to Israel; she was detained for 45 days.
SOURCE: [NPR - Tufts Graduate Student Rumeysa Ozturk Released from Immigration Detention](https://www.npr.org/2025/05/09/nx-s1-5393055/tufts-student-rumeysa-ozturk-ordered-freed-from-immigration-detention)

### [2025-04-11] Immigration Judge Rules Mahmoud Khalil Can Be Deported
WHAT: Louisiana immigration judge Jamee Comans ruled Khalil was deportable under Secretary of State Rubio's assertion that his presence posed "adverse foreign policy consequences," and for allegedly omitting organization memberships on his green card application.
SOURCE: [PBS News - Immigration Judge Rules Mahmoud Khalil Can Be Deported](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/politics/immigration-judge-rules-that-columbia-student-mahmoud-khalil-can-be-deported)

### [2025-04-17] 901 International Students File Lawsuits Over Visa Revocations
WHAT: At least 901 international students at 128 colleges and universities filed lawsuits against the Trump administration for denial of due process related to mass SEVIS terminations and visa revocations.
SOURCE: [CNN - Why Are So Many Visas Being Revoked](https://www.cnn.com/2025/04/19/us/visa-revoked-students-trump-ice)

### [2025-05-09] Rumeysa Ozturk Released on Bail After 45 Days
WHAT: A federal judge ordered Ozturk's release from immigration custody, finding her detention for writing an op-ed constituted targeting of protected political speech.
SOURCE: [NPR - Tufts Student Rumeysa Ozturk Ordered Freed from Immigration Detention](https://www.npr.org/2025/05/09/nx-s1-5393055/tufts-student-rumeysa-ozturk-ordered-freed-from-immigration-detention)

### [2025-05-28] Administration Announces Aggressive Revocation of Chinese Student Visas
WHAT: The Trump administration announced it would "aggressively revoke" Chinese student visas in a major escalation with Beijing, broadening the scope of visa enforcement beyond protest-related revocations.
SOURCE: [CNN - Trump Administration Will Aggressively Revoke Chinese Student Visas](https://www.cnn.com/2025/05/28/politics/student-visa-china-revoke-rubio)

### [2025-06-20] Mahmoud Khalil Released on Bail After 3+ Months in Detention
WHAT: Khalil was released from the Louisiana immigration detention center where he had been held since early March, hours after a federal judge in New Jersey ordered the government to release him; his deportation case remains under appeal.
SOURCE: [NPR - Columbia Activist Mahmoud Khalil Released on Bail](https://www.npr.org/2025/06/20/nx-s1-5440351/judge-orders-release-of-columbia-activist-mahmoud-khalil)

### [2025-12-08] Total Visa Revocations Reach 85,000+
WHAT: The State Department confirmed at least 85,000 visas had been revoked since January 2025, with numbers continuing to climb toward 100,000+ by mid-January 2026; approximately 6,000 were student visas.
SOURCE: [CNN - Trump Administration Has Revoked 85,000 Visas Since January](https://www.cnn.com/2025/12/08/politics/visa-revocations-state-department)

### [2025-12-09] Court Orders Restoration of Ozturk's Student Status
WHAT: U.S. District Judge Denise Casper ordered the Trump administration to restore Ozturk's name to the SEVIS database, allowing her to resume research and teaching at Tufts while her immigration case proceeds.
SOURCE: [CNN - Turkish Student Who Criticized Israel Can Resume Research at Tufts](https://www.cnn.com/2025/12/09/us/immigration-tufts-student-detained)

## ICE Enforcement in Communities

### [2025-06-20] ICE Detains Men on Catholic Church Grounds in California
WHAT: ICE agents detained men on the grounds of two Inland Empire Catholic churches -- Our Lady of Lourdes in Montclair and St. Adelaide's in Highland -- prompting the Bishop of San Bernardino to formally lift the obligation to attend Mass for Catholics fearing immigration enforcement.
SOURCE: [CalMatters - ICE Targets Men on Inland Empire Church Grounds](https://calmatters.org/california-divide/2025/07/ice-targets-immigrants-church-grounds/)

### [2025-07-02] ICE Arrests Man During Church Food Distribution Event
WHAT: A Methodist church in Los Angeles reported that ICE arrested a member during a food distribution event, further demonstrating enforcement in formerly protected religious spaces.
SOURCE: [CBS Los Angeles - Methodist Church Says ICE Arrested Member During Food Distribution](https://www.cbsnews.com/amp/losangeles/news/methodist-church-in-los-angeles-says-ice-arrested-member-during-a-food-distribution-event/)

### [2025-08-06] Pastors Describe ICE Raids as "Domestic Terror"
WHAT: Multiple pastors reported ICE enforcement actions near their churches had devastated their congregations, with Hispanic parishioners -- including U.S. citizens and legal residents -- staying home from services out of fear; pastors described the impact as "domestic terror" on faith communities.
SOURCE: [Episcopal News Service - Shaken by ICE Raids, Pastors Rethink Ministries](https://episcopalnewsservice.org/2025/08/06/this-is-domestic-terror-shaken-by-ice-raids-pastors-rethink-ministries/)

### [2025-2026] School Enrollment Drops Due to Immigration Enforcement Fear
WHAT: K-12 enrollment and attendance dropped significantly in immigrant communities nationwide, with Los Angeles and Miami-Dade County public schools both seeing 4% decreases in 2025-2026 enrollment; ICE agents were reported circling school campuses, stalking drop-off lines, and stopping school buses.
SOURCE: [EdTrust - Separating Families, Terrorizing Communities, Upending Schools](https://edtrust.org/blog/ice-raids-upending-schools-communities/)

## Revocation of Legal Status for Hundreds of Thousands

### [2025-03-25] CHNV Humanitarian Parole Termination Announced
WHAT: DHS published a Federal Register Notice terminating the CHNV (Cuban, Haitian, Nicaraguan, Venezuelan) humanitarian parole program, stripping lawful status from approximately 532,000 people who had entered the U.S. legally.
SOURCE: [Refugees International - Explainer on Termination of Parole](https://www.refugeesinternational.org/explainer-on-termination-of-parole/)

### [2025-05-30] Supreme Court Allows CHNV Parole Termination to Proceed
WHAT: The Supreme Court granted the administration's request to pause a lower court order that had blocked the CHNV parole termination, allowing DHS to proceed with sending termination notices to hundreds of thousands of legal parolees.
SOURCE: [Vorys - SCOTUS Allows Trump Administration to Terminate CHNV Parole Program](https://www.vorys.com/publication-scotus-allows-trump-administration-to-terminate-chnv-parole-program-current-status-of-venezuelan-haitian-tps)

### [2025-07-08] TPS Terminated for Honduras and Nicaragua
WHAT: The Trump administration ended Temporary Protected Status for Honduras and Nicaragua, joining Venezuela (both 2021 and 2023 designations) in having TPS revoked, collectively stripping legal status from over 1.5 million immigrants.
SOURCE: [Newsweek - Trump Ends Temporary Legal Status for Over 1.5 Million Immigrants](https://www.visaverge.com/news/trump-ends-temporary-legal-status-for-over-1-5-million-immigrants-in-2025/)

WHAT: The Trump administration weakened ICE's "Detained Parents Directive," removing written commitments to "humane enforcement" and giving agents broad discretion over family separations; the revised guidance only asks agents to "remain cognizant of the impact enforcement actions may have on a minor child."

### [2025-09-23] More Than 100 U.S. Citizen Children Separated from Parents

## Due Process Violations & Expedited Removal

### [2025-01-20] Expanded Expedited Removal Nationwide
WHAT: The administration authorized ICE to place anyone into expedited removal proceedings (deportation without a judicial hearing) if they could not prove two years of continuous U.S. presence, regardless of where they were encountered -- expanding a process previously limited to border areas to the entire U.S. interior.
SOURCE: [Migration Policy Institute - Trump's Expansion of Fast-Track Deportation Powers](https://www.migrationpolicy.org/article/trump-expedited-removal)

### [2025-04-07] Supreme Court Finds Trump Violated Due Process in Alien Enemies Act Deportations
WHAT: In an apparent 7-2 ruling, the Supreme Court found the administration violated the due process rights of Venezuelan migrants by providing only ~24 hours notice before deportation under the Alien Enemies Act, "devoid of information about how to exercise due process rights to contest that removal."
SOURCE: [Courthouse News - Supreme Court Finds Trump Violated Migrant Due Process Rights](https://www.courthousenews.com/supreme-court-says-trump-violated-migrants-due-process-rights-keeping-pause-on-deportations-under-wartime-authority/)

### [2025-08-2025] Federal Judge Blocks Expanded Expedited Removal
WHAT: A federal judge in Washington, D.C. blocked the expansion of expedited removal into the U.S. interior, finding the policy violated constitutional due process rights, and separately blocked application of expedited removal to parolees.
SOURCE: [ACLU - Federal Court Blocks Trump Administration Fast-Track Deportation Policy](https://www.aclu.org/press-releases/federal-court-blocks-trump-administration-fast-track-deportation-policy)

## Detention System Expansion & Deaths

### [2025, Full Year] ICE Detention Population Surges to Record Levels
WHAT: The number of people in immigration detention rose from ~40,000 in January 2025 to nearly 70,000 by January 2026 -- a 75% increase and the highest level in U.S. history; ICE added 104 new facilities (a 91% increase) and Congress passed $45 billion for detention expansion.
SOURCE: [American Immigration Council - Immigration Detention Expansion in Trump's Second Term](https://www.americanimmigrationcouncil.org/report/immigration-detention/)

### [2025, Full Year] Detention Deaths Reach 20-Year High
WHAT: At least 31 people died in ICE custody in 2025, nearly tripling the 11 deaths in 2024 and reaching a two-decade high; causes included medical neglect, suicide, and conditions linked to overcrowding; December 2025 was the deadliest month on record.
SOURCE: [Axios - Immigrant Detention Deaths Reach 20-Year High Under Trump](https://www.axios.com/2026/01/20/ice-custody-deaths-trump-surge)

### [2025, Full Year] Detention Conditions Deteriorate
WHAT: Detainees across facilities reported poor-quality food, extreme temperatures, permanent lighting, limited clean water access, denial of insulin and disability accommodations, blocked access to lawyers, and sewage bubbling from bathroom drains.
SOURCE: [American Immigration Council - Immigration Detention Is Harsher and Less Accountable Than Ever](https://www.americanimmigrationcouncil.org/press-release/report-trump-immigration-detention-2026/)

## Enforcement Numbers & Statistics

### [2025, Full Year] ICE Arrests Surge to ~1,100 Per Day
WHAT: ICE arrests averaged 821 per day between January and October 2025, surging to roughly 1,100 per day by late 2025; "at-large" arrests in communities (as opposed to at the border) increased by 600%, and the number of people with no criminal record in ICE detention increased by 2,450%.
SOURCE: [Axios - New Data: ICE Arrests Surge as Agency Chases Trump Quota](https://www.axios.com/2025/12/04/trump-ice-immigration-arrests-deportations)

### [2025, Full Year] Deportation Numbers Disputed
WHAT: DHS claimed over 675,000 removals and 2.2 million self-deportations by January 2026; independent analysis by TRAC (Syracuse University) counted approximately 290,603 actual removals, finding the government inflated numbers by including other enforcement categories.
SOURCE: [NPR - Evidence Shows DHS Claims About Deportations Since January Are Not Accurate](https://www.npr.org/2025/11/09/nx-s1-5599987/evidence-shows-dhs-claims-about-deportations-since-january-are-not-accurate)

### [2025, Full Year] 70% of Detained Immigrants Lack Legal Representation
WHAT: An estimated 70% of people in immigration detention had no legal representation, and those subject to expedited removal were effectively unable to consult with counsel, present evidence, or contest their cases before a judge.
SOURCE: [Vera Institute - Weaponizing the System: One Year of Trump's Attacks on Due Process](https://www.vera.org/explainers/weaponizing-the-system-one-year-of-trumps-attacks-on-due-process)

### [2026-01-03] ICE Workforce Increased by 120%
WHAT: ICE announced a 120% increase in its workforce, adding more than 12,000 officers and agents, enabling broader and more aggressive enforcement operations across the country.
SOURCE: [Kutak Rock LLP - Recent ICE Enforcement Activity](https://www.kutakrock.com/newspublications/publications/2026/february/ice-enforcement-rapid-response-preparedness)
